How to use the di.me prototype

Please try the typical things you can do in the prototype.

If you have an Android smartphone, you can use the browser or an Android App:

Install the di.me App for Android
  • On your smartphone: Download the app to your Android device:

                 
download the apk

  • On Android: Look for the download in the notifications
  • Klick on the download file "di.me-mobile.apk".
  • Follow the Android installation instruction. Please note: You have to accept installations from "unknown sources", because di.me was not installed from the App store.
  • Start the app: Go to "All apps" and klick on "di.me mobile"

Before you start: For our project we need to collect anonymous evaluation data when you use the prototype. If you have disagreed to that in the registration, we kindly ask you to accept now:

Accept to send us evaluation data
  • In di.me: Go to "Settings", select "General Settings"
  • Activate the checkbox "send evaluation data"  → Data are recorded when you work with the system. The data are anonymous and do not contain any content, e.g. documents. For details please see xxxx

1. Social networking with different identities

Central to di.me is to use different identities: You can show different ProfilceCards to others and use them for sharing information.

Take a look at your predefined Profile Cards
  • Go to "My Profile Cards"   → Predefined Cards are shown
  • Click on "MyBusinessCard"   → The details with your information are shown.
Use "MyBusinessCard" to share "digital.me_project.pdf" with "TestUser1"
  • Go to "My Data"
  • Select "digital.me_project_flyer.pdf" (browser: use the little box in the corner)
  • Click "Share" (browser: buttons bar on the right)
  • In the pop-up window: Click "Select Profile" and select "MyBusinessCard"
  • Click "Receivers" and select "TestUser1"
  • Click "OK"
Now share something anonymously with “MyAnonymousCard”
  • As above: Select the file "digital.me_logo.jpg", and open the dialog "Share"
  • Click "Select Profile" and select "MyAnonymousCard"
  • Select "TestUser3" as receiver and click "OK"

→

The recepients get the different files. As senders, they see different identities: Testuser1 gets a file from a sender with your information in "MyBusinessCard", Testuser2 receives a file from a sender and the information in "MyAnonymousCard".

2. Try out di.me’s privacy recommendations

A research objective of di.me is to give recommendations on privacy. In di.me, documents have privacy levels, and persons have trust levels. Di.me gives suggestions based on them:

Take a look at the people’s trust and the privacy of files
  • Go to "MyData", click on the file "hiking.jpg"   → The details for the file are shown; the privacy of this file is "high".
  • Go to "People"
  • Select "Test User 3" (use the little box in the corner)
  • Open "Test User 3" (click "edit" in the action bar on the right)   → The details for the person are shown; the trust of this person is "high"
Share the files in “FriendsBox” to the group “Friends”
  • Go to "My Data"
  • Select the data-box "FriendsBox" (use the little box in the corner)
  • Share the file to "Test User 3" (click "Share" in the action bar on the right, select a Profile Card and "Test User 3")

→ di.me shows the warning that the photo “hiking.jpg” is private and “TestUser1” has low trust.

→ di.me will suggest to change the trust or privacy values, if you share private items several times to a person with low trust.

Now share the “FriendsBox” with the group “BusinessContacts”
  • As above: Select the data-box "FriendsBox", share it to the group "BusinessContacts"

→ di.me shows the warning that the “FriendsBox” is shared outside the usual groups. Because you shared it to “Friends” before, di.me assumes that sharing it to another group could be wrong.

→

di.me shows recommendations based on privacy of files, and trust of people. Di.me suggests to adapt these values, if they do not fit to what the user shares.
Other recommendations are shown based on di.me’s information model, e.g. if a ProfileCard is revealed the first time, if many items are shared, or if many people get something.

3. Connect to other people

Add other people (who have registered to di.me)
  • Go to "People"
  • Enter the name of people who are already registered to di.me. (Note: use only one search-word, avoid incomplete names).   → search results in your people list and in the public di.me user directory (bottom) are shown.
  • Click "add" to add a person from the di.me user directory to your people
Look for people in the group “di.me project” and send us messages
  • Coming soon: When you register, you are automatically connected to the group "di.me project". In this group, you find people from the research project's consortium.

4. Add information to di.me and share them

You can upload own information to your di.me to share them to others. Your data are then stored on your di.me, i.e. on the server you have registered on (e.g. of the di.me test trial 2013 or your own server if you host di.me yourself).

Upload files to your server and share them
  • Go to "MyData"
  • Upload files (browser: drop a file to the dropzone or use the button "upload" (bottom)
  • Now you can share the file to others, set it's privacy value, etc.
  • You may also use "data boxes" to organize your files: add boxes and drop files in it.
Connect your Linkedin or twitter account and use the profile information from there
  • Go to "Settings"
  • Click on "add a services", select "Linkedin" or "Twitter"
  • Follow the instructions of the dialogs to connect to your accounts.

→ Contacts from Linkedin are synchronized to your di.me. Di.me shows a suggestion to merge these contacts to existing ones that you perhaps already have.

→ Your profile information from Linkedin or Twitter is imported to di.me. You can use this for enriching your di.me profile cards.
