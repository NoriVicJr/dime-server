Guided Tour

This tour will show you the prototype and demonstrate the main concepts of the di.me research project. You can either use your favourite Internet Browser, or the di.me Android App if you have an Android Smartphone:

Installing the di.me App for Android
  • On your smartphone: Download the app to your Android device:

                 
download the apk

  • Look for the download in the Android notifications
  • Klick on the download file "di.me-mobile.apk".
  • Follow the Android installation instruction. Please note: You have to accept installations from "unknown sources", because di.me was not installed from the App store.
  • Start the app: Go to "All apps" and klick on "di.me mobile"
  • Now you can login to your di.me account on the Android client.

Before you start: For research purposes we need to collect anonymised usage data. If you have disagreed to participate at registration time, we kindly ask you to accept that now:

Accept to send us evaluation data
  • In di.me: Go to "Settings", select "General Settings"
  • The data is anonymous and does not contain any content, e.g. documents. Under "Further information", please read the details which evaluation data are collected.
  • Activate the checkbox "Send anonymous click data"

1. Social networking with different identities

Central to di.me is the concept of using different identities: You can create Profile Cards and reveal different ones when sharing information with others.

Take a look at your predefined Profile Cards
  • Go to "My Profile Cards"
      → Predefined Cards are shown.
  • Click on "MyBusinessCard"
      → The selected personal details are shown. Please note: When registering, your information is copied in some Profile Cards by default.
Use "MyBusinessCard" to share "digital.me_project.pdf" with "TestUser1"
  • Go to "My Data"
  • Select "digital.me_project_flyer.pdf" (browser: use the little box in the top-right corner)
  • Click "Share" (browser: bar on the right)
  • In the pop-up window: Click "Select Profile" and select "MyBusinessCard"
  • Click "Receivers" and select "TestUser1"
  • Click "OK"
Now share something anonymously with "MyAnonymousCard"
  • As above: Select the file "digital.me_logo.jpg", and open the dialog "Share"
  • Click "Select Profile" and select "MyAnonymousCard"
  • Select "TestUser3" as receiver and click "OK"

→

The recepients get the different files. As senders, they see different identities: Testuser1 receives a file from a person showing the information in your "MyBusinessCard", whereas Testuser3 receives a file from a person showing the information in your "MyAnonymousCard".

2. Try out di.me’s privacy recommendations

One of di.me’s research objectives is to give privacy recommendations. In di.me, documents have privacy levels, and persons have trust levels. Di.me gives suggestions based on both:

Take a look at person trust and file privacy levels
  • Go to "MyData", click on file "hiking.jpg"
      → The details for the file are shown; its privacy is set to "high".
  • Go to "People".
  • Open "TestUser3" (browser: select the little box on "TestUser3", click "edit" in the bar on the right)
      → The details for the person are shown; the trust level for this person is set to "high".
Share the files in "FriendsBox" with the "Friends" group
  • Go to "My Data"
  • Select the "FriendsBox" databox (browser: select the little box in the top-right corner)
  • Share the databox with "Friends" (browser: click "Share" in the bar on the right, select any Profile Card and choose "Friends")
    → di.me shows a warning because "hiking.jpg" is private and "TestUser1" has low trust.
    → di.me will suggest to change the trust or privacy values, if you share private items several times to a person with low trust.
Now share the "FriendsBox" with the "BusinessContacts" group
  • As above: Select the "FriendsBox", share it with "BusinessContacts"
    → di.me shows a warning because "FriendsBox" is being shared outside the usual groups. Since you shared it with "Friends" before, di.me warns that sharing it to another group could be unintended.

→

di.me shows recommendations based on file privacy, and contact trust levels. di.me will suggest to adapt them.
Other recommendations are shown when there is a potentially unintentional sharing of data, e.g. if a Profile Card is revealed for the first time, if many items are shared all at once, or if the user is sharing an item with a lot of people.

3. Connecting to other people

Look for other people, e.g. from the di.me project
  • Go to "People":
      → A group of persons from the di.me project is automatically in your contact list.

  • Search in the public di.me user directory (browser: enter a name in the search field; Please Note: use e.g. "John" or "Doe", but not "John Doe", or "Jo", or "Do").
      → Search results obtained from your contact list and from the public di.me user directory (bottom) are shown.
  • Click "add" to add the desired person from the di.me user directory to your di.me contacts.
Send a message to someone

(browser: Please note: The steps for writing a message are currently quite inconvenient... a better flow is coming soon!)

  • Go to "liveposts" (browser: click the letter-icon in the top menu)
  • Click "new livepost" (browser: in the bar on the right)
  • Enter a message text, click "Submit"
      → A new livepost appears in the list
  • As above: Select the livepost, click "Share", and select the recepients

4. Add your own data to di.me and share it

You can upload your own files and information to your di.me, in order to share them with your contacts. Your data is stored on your di.me, i.e. on the server to which you have registered (the di.me test trial 2013 or your own server if you opted to host di.me yourself).

Upload files to your di.me server and share them
  • Go to "MyData"
  • Upload files (browser: drop a file to the dropzone or use the button "upload" at the bottom)
  • Now you can share the file with others, set it's privacy value, etc.
  • You may also use "data boxes" to organize your files: add your own boxes and drop your files in it.
Connect your Linkedin or Twitter account and see the profile information from there
  • Go to "Settings"
  • Click on "add a services", then select "Linkedin" or "Twitter"
  • Follow the instructions of the dialogs to connect to your accounts.
    → Contacts from Linkedin are shown in your di.me contact list. Di.me will also show suggestions to merge contacts to existing ones that you perhaps already have.
    → Your profile details from Linkedin or Twitter are imported to di.me. You can reuse these to enrich your di.me profile cards.
Add new profile details

You can add or remove profile details e.g. from "MyBusinessCard"; if you have connected to Linkedin or Twitter, you can reuse these profile details from there.

  • Go to "My Profile".
  • Click on "MyBusinessCard".
  • Add or remove profile details (browser: Click on the profile details; in the popup window, click on the profile details to add or remove them from the Profile Card).

You can add new profile details.

  • Go to "My Profile".
  • Make a new profile detail(browser: click "New > New profile detail" in the bar on the right)"
  • Select the category, e.g. "PhoneNumber", or "Interest".
  • Add a descriptioin e.g. "Private mobile phone".
  • Add information, e.g. the phone number, or an interest, and click OK → After a while, the new profile detail shows up in the list.
  • As above: Now you can add this new profile detail to a Profile Card, e.g. "MyBusinessCard"
Add a new profile card
  • Go to "My Profile".
  • Make a new Profile Card (browser: click "New > New Profile Card" in the bar on the right)"
  • Enter a title for the Card, e.g. "MyCloseFriendsCard"
  • As above: Add or remove profile information (browser: Click on the list of attributes; in the popup window, click on the attributes to add or remove them from the Profile Card).

5. di.me's context-awareness

di.me is a context-aware system: You can try out the detection of locations, situations, people nearby, etc.:

Prepare your di.me Android app: Send location and sensor data to your di.me server.

The context-aware functions in di.me need location and sensor data from your mobile phone. The data is stored on your di.me server and used by di.me services (see below).

  • On Android: Go to "Settings"
  • Click on "Store Android sensor data"
See your situations, or add custom ones.

You can train di.me to recognize your siguations: If you train the system, di.me will recognize the situation according to your context.

  • Click on the current situation (browser: in the top-right corner below your user-name; Android: on the start page)
      → The list of situations provided by default is shown
  • click on "activate (browser: in the bar on the right)
      → The selected situation is now active and the system learns that it is related to the current sensor data.
      → As you use and train di.me to recognise your situations, you will notice that the order of the stored situations will change according to your context.
